Flat vs. Skeuo by Madeleine Deason

by Madeleine Deason
The debate over flat design vs. skeuomorphism is ongoing. And though flat design has been trendy recently, is it the best option in terms of usability?

Flat Design: Pros

  • Simple & clean
  • Modern & trendy
  • Straightforward presentation of content
  • Focus on functionality
  • Reliable user experience
  • Easy to make resposive

Flat Design: Cons

  • Calls to action are less clear
  • Can limit creativitiy
  • Can get boring or repetitive; hard to be original
  • A trend that is already outdated (all hail material design)

Skeuomorphism: Pros

  • Calls to action are clear
  • Familiar design based on the physical world
  • Quickly establishes the purpose of the site

Skeuomorphism: Cons

  • Not trendy; dated
  • Doesn't consider digital usability
  • Can look cluttered or tacky

So which is it?

Both are at extreme ends of the spectrum, so it's probably not a good idea to rely soley on either. Good design is more than what's the trendiest or most familiar. Good design must serve the user.
